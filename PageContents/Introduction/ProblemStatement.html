Problem Statement


In today’s society, building social connections via virtual applications has become standard. Especially after COVID-19, people have become more reliant on virtual applications to not only build new connections but also to be able to interact with other people. As people become more reliant on these online applications, feelings of detachment and isolation can start to build. These feelings of detachment and isolation start to build as virtual connections lack the emotional and expressive experience that face-to-face connections have.

Virtual connections allow for people to form new connections a lot easier than meeting people face-to-face, but are limited in terms of the connection that can form between people. We have recognized the importance of being able to convey authentic and emotional expressions when building new connections for people and communities alike. In virtual environments, people are more likely to not be able to view expressions in comparison to face-to-face interaction. This leads to the lack of emotional and expressive experience that we found as someone may seem different behind a screen compared to face-to-face and not completely be able to convey one’s whole character.
